# Inventory vs Communications Diff — Pass 2 GDPR Audit
**Date:** 2026-05-22
**Inputs:** `docs/audit/data-inventory-2026-05-22.md` (Pass 1, code-derived inventory)
**Scope:** Formal legal docs (DPIA, RoPA, retention policy, privacy_architecture), website pages (privacy policy, terms, public privacy explainer, consumer landing branch), in-app i18n strings, ConsentBottomSheet, DataPrivacyScreen, WelcomeScreen, SocialRegistrationScreen, PermissionPrimingScreen, iOS `Info.plist`, `app.json`, Android `AndroidManifest.xml`.
**Methodology:** Read-only comparison. No source code, doc, or config files were edited.

---

## 1. Summary

| Severity | Count |
|----------|-------|
| **High** | 6 |
| **Medium** | 11 |
| **Low** | 9 |
| **Total** | 26 |

**Top finding (one-line):** The public privacy explainer at `website/src/pages/privacy.astro` (EN) lists **"Hospital or employer name"** under "Not collected" — directly contradicting the inventory (B.1.1, C.3), which shows `hospital_id`, `hospital_ref_id`, and a hospital name dropdown are explicitly collected and transmitted to the backend; the German equivalent correctly admits this, so the EN page is materially misleading users about a core fact of the data model.

Two structural problems cut across many findings:
1. **Social authentication is undisclosed everywhere except the auth UI itself.** No legal document (DPIA, RoPA, retention policy, privacy policy, terms) mentions Apple/Google Sign-In, the JWKS calls to `appleid.apple.com` / `googleapis.com`, the `provider_sub` storage, or the email-discard behaviour. Users granting consent via the social path are accepting policies that don't describe their data flow.
2. **The feedback / bug-report pipeline is completely undisclosed** — no surface tells users that "Report Issue" sends ~100 recent geofence events with GPS accuracy stats, location names, device model, app version, OS version, and (optionally) hospital_id, specialty, role_level, state_code to the backend.

## 6. Notable strengths (where surfaces are *better* than typical apps)

These are areas where existing copy and disclosure are above industry average and should be preserved:

- **Consent flow is a real bottom sheet** (`ConsentBottomSheet.tsx`) with 4 plain-language key points, links to full Terms + Privacy, and a separate "How your data is protected" link to a non-legal privacy explainer. Far better than a "By tapping Continue you agree…" footer. (`mobile-app/src/modules/auth/components/ConsentBottomSheet.tsx`, copy in `en.ts:336-360` / `de.ts:336-360`)
- **`settings.calendarSyncDescription`** (en.ts:443-444, de.ts:443-444) is a model disclosure: it explicitly warns "If that calendar syncs with iCloud/Google or is shared, those events may appear there too" — anticipates an indirect data flow and discloses it inline at the relevant control.
- **`dataPrivacyScreen.privacyInfo`** (en.ts:730, de.ts:729) is a self-contained accurate description of what's local vs sent — accessible inside Settings without needing to open a browser.
- **Account deletion is in-app, one tap from `DataPrivacyScreen.tsx:411-420`** with clear "permanent" warnings + pending-data warning if there are queued weeks. The flow correctly cascades to local cleanup, geofence unregistration, and biometric reset (lines 230-269). Above average — many apps require email to support.
- **Data export is in-app and immediate** via `GET /auth/me/export` (`DataPrivacyScreen.tsx:162-184`) with JSON output and native share sheet. Art. 20 portability done well.
- **`data-retention-policy.md`** has a concrete deletion message (Appendix) and explicit handling for the backup-window edge case — most retention policies are vague here.
- **DPIA's R7** (`docs/DPIA.md:252-266`) honestly acknowledges that profile-data combinations could enable re-identification at small hospitals, and flags planned mitigations. The DPIA is more honest than the user-facing privacy explainer about this risk.
- **The "Aggregated statistics are retained after account deletion" caveat is explained** in every layer — privacy policy, consent sheet, retention policy, and the post-deletion success message (`retention-policy.md:233-240`). Many platforms omit this.
- **No advertising IDs, no analytics SDKs, no third-party tracking** — `Info.plist` and `AndroidManifest.xml` confirm; `data-retention-policy.md:34` documents.
- **Permission priming screens** (`PermissionPrimingScreen.tsx`) include a dedicated privacy note box (line 59-65) on each priming step — far better than going straight to the system dialog.
- **Yellow "Draft — pending legal review" banners** are visibly present on both privacy policy and terms pages (EN + DE) — sets honest expectations during the pre-launch period.
